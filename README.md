# Bilingual Stray Birds · 双语飞鸟集

A parallel-text digital edition of Rabindranath Tagore's *Stray Birds* (1916), presenting the English and Chinese on facing pages with selected vocabulary annotation.

泰戈尔《飞鸟集》(1916) 的中英对照数字版，英文原文与中文译文左右并置，并附选词注释。

> 🌐 **在线阅读 / Live demo**: https://zynieie.github.io/bilingual-stray-birds/

## English

### About the book

*Stray Birds* was first published in 1916. Three hundred and twenty-six short poems, the shortest just a single line, the longest never more than two. Tagore was convalescing in Japan when he wrote them. A bird flew past his window and he set down a sentence. Then another. Then three hundred and twenty-five more.

The whole book moves like that. A flower, a child, the rain, dusk, God, a woman's face. No long arguments, no rising and falling action — just a moment of seeing, frozen in a line. Take poem 1, the very first:

> *Stray birds of summer come to my window to sing and fly away.*

Or poem 6, which I keep coming back to:

> *If you shed tears when you miss the sun, you also miss the stars.*

Tagore won the Nobel Prize in 1913 for *Gitanjali*, becoming the first non-European laureate. *Stray Birds* was not the prize-winning book, but it is probably the one most people in East Asia actually read — partly because it is so light, and partly because the Chinese translator Zheng Zhenduo (郑振铎) brought it across in 1922 and it lodged itself in the modern Chinese imagination for good.

### Why bilingual

I first read *Stray Birds* in Chinese as a teenager, in an anthology. Years later I went looking for the English and discovered there were several "original" versions in circulation. In the early ones, Tagore deliberately capitalised certain words — *STRAY*, *TROUPE*, *THE mighty desert* — as if to stencil a silhouette against the sky. Most Chinese translations drop that. Most modern English reprints normalise the spelling. Both choices are defensible, but once you know the original typography exists, you can't un-see it.

So this reader puts the two side by side: English on the left, Chinese on the right. No annotation insists on one meaning. You can read either first, or both at once, or jump back and forth when a translation feels off.

A small vocabulary list sits under each poem. It is not exhaustive — just the words that aren't obvious from context, with IPA and a short gloss. Read past it if you don't need it.

### What you can do in the reader

- Read the English and Chinese of each poem side by side.
- Search across all 326 poems by English or Chinese text.
- Filter to a smaller subset if you don't want to scroll the full collection.
- Mark poems as favourites.
- Pick an English typeface and a Chinese typeface separately — they don't have to be the same family.
- Resize the body text in three steps. Small for skimming, large for a long evening.
- Everything you change is remembered on your device; nothing is sent to a server.

There is no login, no analytics, no backend. The whole thing is a single HTML file.

## 中文

### 关于这本书

《飞鸟集》1916 年首次出版。三百二十六首短诗，最短的一行，最长也不过两行。泰戈尔写这本书的时候正在日本养病。窗外有鸟飞过，他就记下一句。再记一句。再记三百二十五句。

整本书都是这个节奏——一朵花、一个孩子、一场雨、一阵黄昏、一个女人的脸、一尊神。没什么宏大叙事，也不讲究起承转合，只是一个"看见"的瞬间，被定格在两行字里。比如第一首，开篇就是：

> *Stray birds of summer come to my window to sing and fly away.*
> 夏日的迷鸟来到我的窗前，歌唱，然后飞走了。

再比如第六首，我一直记着：

> *If you shed tears when you miss the sun, you also miss the stars.*
> 如果你因思念太阳而流泪，你也会错过星辰。

泰戈尔 1913 年凭《吉檀迦利》获诺贝尔文学奖，是**第一个获此殊荣的非欧洲人**。《飞鸟集》不是获奖作，但很可能是整个东亚读者真正读完的那一本——一半因为它轻，一半因为郑振铎 1922 年的译本把它带进了中国新诗的底色里，至今没退。

### 为什么做双语

我最早是中学时在语文课外读本里读到《飞鸟集》的，当时看的是冰心译本。后来去找英文版，发现"原版"其实不止一种。早年的版本里，泰戈尔故意把一些词大写——`STRAY`、`TROUPE`、`THE mighty desert`——像飞鸟掠过头顶时剪下的轮廓。这种细节，大多数中文译本都放弃了；后来出的英文重排版又把大写统一改回小写。各有各的道理，但一旦知道原版长那样，就再也忘不掉。

所以做了这个阅读器：英文在左，中文在右，**没有哪一边是"正解"**。先读哪边都行，对照着读也行，觉得某个译法不对跳回去也欢迎。

每首诗下面附了一个小词汇表——不是穷尽式的，只挑上下文里看不出来的词，给音标和简短释义。看得懂就跳过，看不懂就翻一下。

### 在阅读器里能做什么

- 中英并排读每首诗
- 在全部 326 首诗里搜英文或中文
- 如果不想一次看完，可以筛选出子集
- 给喜欢的诗加星
- 中英文字体分开切换——不强制是同一族
- 三档正文字号。小字扫读，大字夜读
- 你改过的所有偏好都记在本地，不上传任何地方

没有登录、没有分析、没有后端。整个东西就是一个 HTML 文件。
